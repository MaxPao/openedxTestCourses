ANSWERING QUESTION 2 USING EXCEL 2010

You want to create a relationship between the Sales table and the Locations table. To do this, you need to establish a common field between the two tables. The instructions are to create a new column CountryZip by merging these two columns together. You may have noticed that there is no Merge Columns feature in PowerPivot, however, you can still create this new field by combining the two columns together using the CONCATENATE() function.

You will need to perform this CONCATENATE operation on both tables.

Starting with the Locations table, you will be adding your new column in the area named "Add Column" - you will be entering a Column name (CountryZip) in the first row of this column and the formula in the second row (the formula appears in the formula bar).

Double click into the first row of this Add Column section and type in the header CountryZip and hit your enter key.

Place your cursor in the second row of this new column and begin typing =CONCATENATE - once the formula begins to appear as an option, you can double click it to select it. This formula requires two text fields to combine. While inside the CONCATENATE left parenthesis, click on the country column, add a comma and then click on the Zip column and close the formula off with a right parenthesis. After you completed your formula, press your enter key. [NOTE you could also use the & operator to combine two or more columns together]

Repeat this Concatenate operation for the second table. Once you have completed this for both tables, change to diagram view. I have rearranged my tables to fit all of the tables on the screen and so that the two tables I'm trying to relate are next to each other. One table that isn't related to the other three is called a reference table and will have only one row for each CountryZip combination whereas the second table that you need to relate it to may have many rows with the same CountryZip combination.

Scroll down the field list for both tables until you can see the newly added CountryZip fields you just added. Click on the CountryZip field from the unrelated table to drag it and drop it onto the other table's CountryZip field - this will create the relationship for you.
